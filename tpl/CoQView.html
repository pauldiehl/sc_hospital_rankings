Cost of quality (CoQ) is a summary metric that helps us rank a hospital against its peers. The actual calculation of CoQ is not provided as it does not prove useful to patients in assessing hospitals. However, the percentage difference between the hospital's CoQ and the state average CoQ assists patients in optimizing their hospital selection.

CoQ variance is determined by three calculations:

1) Average total charges for <%= name %> was divided by its total performance score. In other words, we divided the average cost of an individual patient by the quality that the hospital provides.

2) The same calculation was then made at the state level.

3) The percentage difference between the two rates was found in order to produce its current variance.

Outpatient and Inpatient Charge data and Total Performance Scores as of 2010 were extracted from data.medicare.gov/data/hospital-compare.

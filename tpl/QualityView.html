Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) provides regular evaluations on CMS-accredited hospitals for the purpose of assessing quality. The overall scoring each hospital receives is reported in CMS's Hospital Value Based-Purchasing (HVBP) chart in the Total Performance Score (TPS).

The Total Performance Score (TPS) is derived from three domains in FY 2014—Clinical Process of Care, Patient Experience of Care and Outcome Domains.

The Clinical Process of Care domain is comprised of 13 clinical process measures and accounts for 45 percent of a hospital’s TPS. The Patient Experience of Care domain is composed of 8 dimensions derived from the HCAHPS Survey and accounts for 30 percent of a hospital’s TPS. The Outcome Domain contains 3 mortality measures and accounts for 25 percent of a hospital’s TPS.

To calculate the quality variance measure, we simply found the percentage difference between the TPS for <%= name %> and the state average TPS.

TPS data as of 2014 was extracted from medicare.gov/hospitalcompare/Data/total-performance-scores.html.
